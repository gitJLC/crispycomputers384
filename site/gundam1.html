What are the differences between the gundam grades?

Nov 30th 2024 short read

Gundam model kits, also known as Gunpla (short for Gundam Plastic Model), are categorized into different grades that signify their complexity, size, and level of detail. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced builder, understanding these grades can help you choose a kit that matches your skill level and preferences. Here’s an overview of the most popular grades:

High Grade (HG)

High Grade kits are one of the most popular choices for beginners and casual builders. They are typically 1/144 scale, making them compact and easy to display. HG kits feature decent articulation and detail, often using fewer parts than more advanced grades. They are ideal for quick builds and experimentation with painting or customization. Despite being budget-friendly, they still offer impressive designs that capture the essence of various Gundam series.

Real Grade (RG)

For builders seeking a more intricate and detailed model, Real Grade kits offer a step up. Also at 1/144 scale, RG kits include advanced articulation, more intricate part separation, and color accuracy. They often feature an inner frame for improved poseability, giving them a premium feel despite their smaller size. RG kits are perfect for those who want high detail without the space commitment of larger kits.

Master Grade (MG)

Master Grade kits are aimed at more experienced builders. At a 1/100 scale, they are larger than HG and RG kits, allowing for enhanced detail and better articulation. MG kits include inner frames that provide stability and greater poseability. They also come with optional decals for added realism. These kits are more challenging to assemble but are rewarding for hobbyists looking for an intricate and detailed build.

Perfect Grade (PG)

Perfect Grade kits represent the pinnacle of Gunpla engineering. At a 1/60 scale, these models are larger and include exceptional detail, advanced articulation, and comprehensive inner mechanics. PG kits are designed for dedicated hobbyists due to their higher price point and longer assembly times. They often include LED components and other premium features, making them the ultimate centerpiece for any collection.

Entry Grade (EG)

Entry Grade kits are designed for absolute beginners. These kits are simplified, requiring no tools or glue for assembly. They prioritize ease of use while still offering a fun and satisfying build. Though less detailed than HG kits, they provide an excellent starting point for those new to Gunpla.

Super Deformed (SD)

Super Deformed kits are a whimsical departure from the realistic proportions of other grades. These chibi-style models feature exaggerated, cartoonish designs. They are smaller in scale and easier to build, making them great for younger builders or collectors looking for something unique and playful.

Choosing the Right Grade

Selecting the right grade depends on your skill level, budget, and display space. Beginners might start with HG or EG kits, while seasoned builders might gravitate toward MG or PG kits for their complexity and detail. For those looking to strike a balance between size and intricacy, RG kits are an excellent choice.

No matter which grade you choose, Gunpla offers endless possibilities for creativity and enjoyment, making it a beloved hobby for fans worldwide.
